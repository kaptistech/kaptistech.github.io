Mission 

Here at Kaptis, we have a vision. That vision is to solve climate change, however it may manifest.  Th e greatest factor affecting climate change are man-made carbon emissions. 

Climate change is bad— that’s understood by everyone. The real issue is the way we deal with it.  Green energy does not have the potential to reverse carbon damage in the atmosphere but carbon capture does. Carbon capture is expensive and as a result, it is not favoured financially. 

At Kaptis, we change this. With our technology, we transform carbon dioxide into the high-value substance of graphene. 

If global temperatures are not kept within 1.5–2°C pre-industrial levels, further destruction to our planet such as mass flooding and drought will threaten to ensue. We require carbon-negative methods that remove CO2 from our atmosphere. 

The Process

 Our reactor harnesses plasmonics; the ability for electromagnetic radiation to resonate electrons at the surface of a metal in the air. These interactions create energy to catalyze chemical reactions on the metal surface, therefore harnessing energy at room temperature and saving money. 

Our materials are cheaper than what is used in conventional Carbon Capture but produce the same output. Instead of using palladium like our competitors, we use materials that do not need to be excessively heated to work. 

Our reactor will use copper grating on a titanium dioxide catalyst. Copper has comparable plasmonic properties to silver and 10% as costly. These material costs don’t affect the recurring cost of graphene production per tonne and set our conservative estimate to $40/tonne of CO2.

How can I help?

Answer. We have written an experimental proposal and received feedback from 5+ experts. This includes Professors at Ryerson University, University of Massachusetts, Amherst, and more, as well as founders of carbon capture startups as reference points for advice and iterations. If you have connections to early-stage incubators, labs and academics in the carbon capture space, we'd love to talk with them!

What is next?

Answer.

We have lofty goals for expanding carbon capture, and our first step to achieve these is to conduct an experiment to prove our concept. We are to rent out a part of laboratory space and purchase equipment/catalyst materials in an academic setting. We would use the experiment to determine the yield of our solid carbon/graphite/graphene output from the reaction and the quality of the material, whether copper metal and titanium dioxide catalyst can effectively catalyze reactions, and the amount of light energy/irradiation rate needed to set off the reactions.
